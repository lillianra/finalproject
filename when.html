Mayan Scripts


      When                                                                                                         

            
             The Mayan began their writing system during the second half of the Middle Preclassic period ~ 300 BCE. The language reached full maturity and flourished throughout 
             the Classic period -- 250-900 CE. The best preserved examples of Maya writing are often from stone monuments, most commonly altars, architectural sculpture, 
             especially around doorways and stairs. Pottery is another important source of script because a lot of pots have either painted on or inscribed writing. Finally, 
             there are written books. These books are written on bark paper. In the 9th century CE, they became the preferred medium for Mayan writers as inscriptions on monuments 
             started to disappear.
